## Overview

Today we arrive at the heart chakra of the Mental Week of Aries. The topic is the question **Which?**

These days many important traditions are happening across the world:

Now let's explore how the question "Which?" relates to our heart and the choices we make.

## Etymology of "Which"

### Two Roots

"Which" has a couple of different origins in European language:

1. In the Latin north: **qualican** (in Spanish: **cual**, in Portuguese: **qual**, in Italian: **quale**)
2. In the Germanic: **wich**

### Meaning: Shape and Form

Both come from the same root idea: **which**, **qualican**.

This word is related to **quality** and to **shape and form**.

When you ask "which?" you're asking about the **shape or form of something**, the **quality of something**.

### Quality Comes from Qualican

The word **quality** comes from **qualican**.

Quality is the characteristic that something has according to its form, according to how it is, according to its quality.

### What vs. Which

- **What?** is about the **object** itself
- **Which?** is about the **quality** of the object

---

## Which: Choosing Between Similar Things

### The Function of "Which?"

When there are many things that look the same, that are equal—when you have two or more things similar to each other—you ask: **Which one?**

You need to define which one of those similar things you want.

### Example

If you go into a coffee shop and they have one hundred coffees:
- You don't ask **"What?"** (you know they're all coffees)
- You ask **"Which one?"** (Which coffee do I want?)

### About Quality, Not Quantity

"Which?" is about identifying the **quality** of what you're choosing among similar options.

---

## Two Ways of Asking "Which?"

### From the Ego: Which to Choose?

When you ask from the ego, the question is: **Which one should I choose?**

This comes from the mental mind trying to figure out which thing is better than another, which one fits your goals, which one serves your purposes.

### From the Being: Which Has the Quality I Need?

When you ask from the being, the question is: **Which one has the quality that I need?**

This comes from the heart—from resonance, from attraction, from what you're lacking.

### The Difference

- Ego asks: Which is better for my plans?
- Being asks: Which resonates with what I need to become?

---

## The Law of Attraction

### Misunderstanding Attraction

We usually think the **Law of Attraction** means: you can **wish for whatever you want** and it will come to you.

But that's not how it works.

### Attraction as Tuning

The Law of Attraction is about **tuning**.

Like a radio: you tune to a specific frequency, and that frequency comes through.

You cannot tune to rock music and receive classical music.

### The Heart as Magnet

Your **heart** is the organ that attracts.

The heart is a **magnet**.

The heart has an electromagnetic field that attracts things that have the same frequency as you—or things that have a **different** frequency that you need.

---

## We Fall in Love with What We Don't Have

### The Truth About Love

We don't fall in love with people who have what we have.

We fall in love with people who have **what we don't have**.

### Why?

Because we need to **evolve**.

We need to learn new things, to become something different than what we are.

### The Heart Knows

That's why your heart takes you to a person completely different than you.

Your heart is looking for the **qualities you lack**.

### From the Ego vs. From the Heart

If you choose from the ego, you might choose someone similar to you (safe, predictable).

If you choose from the heart, you'll be attracted to someone different (challenging, evolutionary).

---

## Diversity Is Essential

### Variety and Difference

The question "Which?" appears when there is **variety**, when there is **difference**.

Without variety, we cannot ask "Which?"

### Diversity Allows Evolution

If there were no diversity, we couldn't evolve.

We couldn't find what we're lacking.

We couldn't learn from what is different.

### Honoring Difference

That's why it's essential to honor **diversity**—in people, cultures, ideas, paths.

Difference is not a problem. Difference is an **opportunity** for growth.

---

## Downloading Attributes

### How We Learn

When you see someone doing something you don't know how to do, your brain starts to **download** that information.

You start to **imitate** them.

This is how children learn—by imitating.

### Adults Too

Adults do the same thing, though we're not always conscious of it.

We see someone with a quality we admire, and we start to integrate it.

### This Is Natural Evolution

This process is **natural**.

It's not copying. It's **evolution through resonance**.

---

## Hypocrisy vs. Evolution

### What Is Hypocrisy?

**Hypocrisy** comes from the Greek **hypokrites**, meaning **actor**—someone who pretends to be someone they're not.

When you imitate someone but **forget yourself**, that's hypocrisy.

You become a copy instead of an evolved version of yourself.

### What Is Evolutionary Imitation?

When you imitate someone and **add their qualities to yours**, that's evolution.

You don't replace yourself. You **expand** yourself.

### The Difference

- Hypocrisy: I become you and forget me
- Evolution: I add you to me and become more

### The Question "Which?" Helps

When you ask "Which quality do I need?" you're seeking to **add**, not replace.

You're seeking to evolve, not to copy.

---

## The Practice: Which Quality Do I Need?

### The Task for Today

Look at the people around you—family, friends, colleagues, even strangers.

Ask: **Which quality does that person have that I don't have?**

### Be Specific

Don't just say "they're nice." Say: "They have the quality of patience in conflict" or "They have the quality of speaking truth without fear."

### Why You're Attracted

Then ask: **Why does my heart attract me to that quality?**

What am I lacking? What do I need to learn?

### No Judgment

This isn't about being "less than" someone.

It's about recognizing you're evolving, and different people carry different pieces of what you need to become whole.

## Q&A

### Q: Does this mean I should always choose what's different from me?

Not always. Sometimes you need stability (choose similar). Sometimes you need growth (choose different). The key is to be conscious of which you're doing and why.

### Q: What if I'm attracted to someone with qualities I don't admire?

Then ask: "Which quality behind the one I see do I actually need?" Often what we judge in others is something we're afraid of in ourselves.

### Q: How do I know if I'm being hypocritical or evolutionary when I imitate?

Ask: "Am I adding this to who I am, or replacing who I am?" If you're still yourself plus the new quality, it's evolution. If you've forgotten yourself, it's hypocrisy.

### Q: Isn't the Law of Attraction about manifesting what you want?

It's about tuning to what you already are or what you need to become. You can't manifest classical music if you're tuned to rock. Change your frequency (your being), and you attract what matches.

### Q: What if I don't want to evolve through relationship?

You evolve through everything—not just romantic relationships. Every person, situation, and experience carries qualities you need. You can't avoid evolution.

### Q: How is this different from "opposites attract"?

It's more nuanced. You're attracted to what you lack, but that doesn't mean total opposite. It means complementary qualities that help you become whole.

### Q: What about fasting and the 40-day traditions you mentioned?

Fasting resets the body and clears the heart's electromagnetic field. It helps you tune more clearly to what you actually need versus what the ego wants. Many traditions use it before new beginnings (Easter, Ramadan, new year).
