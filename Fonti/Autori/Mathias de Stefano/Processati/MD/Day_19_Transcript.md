## Overview

Welcome to Day 19, one of the most challenging and potentially shocking teachings in this entire journey. Today we explore the concept of purpose, destiny, and perhaps most confrontingly, the very concept of God itself. This teaching may shake your foundations, and that's exactly the point. Sometimes we must break down old structures before we can build new, more truthful ones.

The information today is linked to our ankles and feet in relation to our path, our destiny, and our purpose in life. The ankles are the joints that allow our feet to move in multiple directions, to adjust to varying terrain, to find balance on uneven ground. They represent flexibility in our approach to the path, the ability to take many ways toward our destination.

The core message from the Higher Self today is radical in its simplicity: Relax. There is no purpose. There is no God—at least not the God we have been taught to believe in. Before you close this book or reject this teaching, understand that this is not nihilism or atheism. This is an invitation to move beyond limited human projections into a much vaster, more truthful understanding of what divine consciousness actually is.

This teaching asks us to examine everything we believe about purpose, meaning, God, and existence itself. It's challenging, potentially disturbing, and absolutely necessary for those who are ready to move beyond spiritual childhood into spiritual maturity.

## Core Teaching

### The Human Projection of God

Throughout history, we as humans have designed God in our own image and resemblance. This is not a controversial statement; it's observable fact. Every culture creates a concept of the divine that reflects its own values, fears, hopes, and understanding. We project our human perspectives onto the infinite and then worship our own projection.

Everything we could have or every idea we could have of what God is, is precisely that: an idea. It's not real; it's a perception. Ideas and perceptions are tools the mind uses to try to understand reality, but they are not reality itself. The map is not the territory. The concept of God is not God.

To understand what is beyond our projections, we have to step outside our perspective. We have to stop looking at the universe from our mammal point of view, our human needs and fears. Consider: does God create plants in the plant's own image? Does God create stones in the stone's image? If everything is a projection from God, why do we insist on humanizing God?

We perceive the universe from our point of view, and the God we believe in doesn't actually exist except as our idea. However, the universe does exist. The universe is real. Therefore, what is true is not the God we believe in (the old man in the sky, the judge, the father figure, the being who has a plan for us) but something much vaster and more mysterious.

### The True Trinity

We can all agree that God is a Holy Trinity. This is found in many traditions and reflects something true about the nature of reality. But we need to understand what trinity really means, beyond religious dogma.

For everything to exist, we need three things. At the atomic level: electron, neutron, proton. At the consciousness level: subconscious, unconscious, and conscious. These three elements are connected in just one spot we call mind—but "mind" is not just the thinking mind. Mind is the "I Am." Mind is everything that exists.

This is like a big cell with three aspects—subconscious, unconscious, and conscious—all working together. What we call "the mind" at these three different levels is actually one mind trying to understand itself constantly. It's looking for thousands, millions of ways to understand itself.

Humans are just one of these ways, one of these attempts at self-understanding. We are the universe trying to understand itself through the particular lens of human consciousness. We are not the pinnacle or the purpose; we are one experiment, one perspective, one mirror in which consciousness can see its own reflection.

### Ourselves as God Trying to Understand Itself

Here's the revolutionary insight: it is ourselves who project the idea of God. It's us, ourselves—God trying to understand itself. Therefore, does God exist? No, not as a separate being. And yes, as the totality of everything that is, including us.

This teaching breaks many people. It shattered structures of belief that felt comforting and secure. Not everyone is ready to hear this. Each person has their own timing, their own readiness. But for those who can receive it, this teaching is profoundly liberating.

The Higher Self is sharing this not to destroy faith but to expand it beyond the limitations of childish beliefs. We are not hoping you understand it immediately. We are simply sharing because you are listening. Otherwise, there would be no point. The only reason we as humans need the image of a God, a plan, someone who decided everything, is because we need a parent.

We as humans need a dad who tells us what to do. We need a mother who says, "Don't worry, relax, I will love you anyway." Mom and dad. Security and unconditional love from an external source because we haven't yet awakened to these qualities within ourselves.

One of the greatest challenges for those of us who are seekers of consciousness is to realize that we are the tools for the divine to know itself. We are not servants of a separate God; we are expressions of consciousness exploring, experiencing, and understanding itself.

### The Universe as Artist Without Purpose

This helps us to understand something profound: the universe is an artist. An artist creates not because there's a purpose or a plan but because creation is the nature of art, of expression, of exploration. The universe doesn't have a purpose in the way we think of purpose. We humans put purpose into our lives. We design our lives with a purpose.

Why do we do this? Because we know we are going to die. When a human realizes they are going to die, we create our lives with the need for a purpose. We need meaning to justify our brief existence. And when we know that our individual life is going to end, we need to create another purpose, a bigger purpose—God, heaven, the afterlife, whatever we want to call it—to assure ourselves that we continue, that there's meaning beyond the individual death.

This is not wrong. It's a natural response to the awareness of mortality. But when we try to understand how the universe actually works, we understand that it is not linear. It's not going upward toward some final goal or purpose. It's circular, cyclical, spiraling. It's a dance, not a march toward a destination.

The universe is playing, exploring, expressing—like an artist lost in the creative process with no need for the creation to "mean" something or achieve some final goal. The meaning is in the process itself, in the exploration, in the infinite creativity and experience.

### The Ankles and Flexibility of Path

The ankles represent our ability to adjust our path, to move in multiple directions, to find balance on uncertain terrain. They give us ways—multiple approaches, various possibilities, different angles from which to move forward.

When we are rigid in our belief that there is one purpose, one right way, one divine plan that we must discover and follow, our ankles become stiff. We lose flexibility. We become fragile, easily thrown off balance when things don't go according to "the plan."

But when we understand that there is no single predetermined purpose, that we are consciousness creating meaning and exploring possibilities, our ankles become supple and strong. We can adjust to any terrain. We can take countless ways forward. We can dance rather than march.

This doesn't lead to meaninglessness or despair. Quite the opposite. It leads to radical freedom and creative responsibility. If there's no external purpose imposed upon us, then we are free to create purpose, to generate meaning, to choose our path moment by moment.

### Manifesting Ideas into Reality

The statement for today's practice is "I Am the Life Manifesting Ideas." This is what we do as conscious beings. We take the formless potential of consciousness and manifest it into ideas, experiences, and realities.

We are not discovering a pre-existing purpose; we are manifesting ideas of purpose. We are not following a predetermined plan; we are creating the plan as we go. We are the artists, just as the universe is an artist. We are the universe expressing its artistic, creative nature through the particular form and perspective of human consciousness.

Every idea we have, every purpose we create, every meaning we find is the universe knowing itself through us. Nothing is imposed from outside. Everything arises from within the one consciousness that is all things.

This is simultaneously humbling and empowering. Humbling because we realize we are not special or chosen for some cosmic mission by an external God. Empowering because we realize we are the creative force itself, conscious expressions of the divine, with the freedom and responsibility to create our experience and contribute to the ever-unfolding exploration of consciousness.

### Beyond the Need for External Authority

The reason this teaching is so challenging is that it removes the comfort of external authority. No God up in the sky telling you what to do. No predetermined plan that ensures everything will work out if you just follow the rules. No ultimate parent taking care of you.

This can feel terrifying at first, like being a child suddenly told there are no adults in charge. But this terror comes from the ego's need for security and its resistance to full responsibility. When we move through the terror, we discover something incredible: we are not children. We are the divine consciousness itself, capable of creating, choosing, and navigating reality with wisdom and love.

We don't need an external God to tell us right from wrong. We have inner knowing, conscience, the natural wisdom of consciousness. We don't need a grand plan to give our lives meaning. We have the capacity to create meaning in every moment through our choices, our love, our creativity, our service.

This is spiritual maturity—not the absence of connection to the divine, but the recognition that we are not separate from it. Not the absence of meaning, but the liberation to create meaning. Not the absence of love, but the recognition that we are love itself seeking to know itself more fully.

## Practice

### Preparation

Sit comfortably and bring awareness to your feet and ankles. Rotate your ankles slowly in circles, first one direction, then the other. Feel the flexibility of these joints, how they allow your feet to move in many directions.

Stand up and walk around for a moment, noticing how your ankles adjust with every step to the surface beneath you. They respond to the terrain without you having to think about it. They allow you to balance, to change direction, to move fluidly through space.

Return to your seat and close your eyes. Take several deep breaths, allowing your mind to become quiet and receptive. Set the intention to explore the concepts of this challenging teaching with an open heart and mind.

### Main Practice: Releasing External Purpose, Embracing Creative Freedom

**Step 1:** Begin by acknowledging any fear, resistance, or discomfort that arose when you heard that there is no external God or predetermined purpose. Don't judge these feelings; simply notice them. Fear of meaninglessness, fear of being alone, fear of having no guidance—whatever is there, acknowledge it with compassion.

**Step 2:** Ask yourself honestly: "Do I believe in God/the Divine because I've experienced it directly, or because I was taught to believe and I'm afraid of what happens if I don't?" There's no wrong answer. Just notice the truth of your current relationship with the concept of God or Divine.

**Step 3:** Now imagine releasing the image of God as an external being—the old man in the sky, the judge, the parent, the being with a plan. Visualize this image dissolving like mist in the morning sun. Notice any grief, relief, fear, or other emotions that arise. Breathe into them.

**Step 4:** In the space where that image was, open your awareness to something much vaster: consciousness itself. Not a being but being. Not a thing but everything. Not separate from you but the very essence of what you are. Rest in this vast, spacious awareness.

**Step 5:** Recognize that you are not a separate self seeking to know God; you are God (consciousness/the universe) seeking to know itself through this particular expression called [your name]. Feel into this. You are the universe experiencing itself subjectively.

**Step 6:** Now ask yourself: "If there is no predetermined purpose imposed upon me, what purpose do I choose to create? What meaning do I choose to manifest?" Don't search for the answer "out there" or wait for a divine voice to tell you. Feel into your own depths, your own knowing, your own creative power. What matters to you? What calls to you?

**Step 7:** Place your hands on your ankles and feel the flexibility there. Say aloud or silently: "I am free to choose my path. I am free to create my purpose. I am consciousness exploring itself through infinite ways." Feel the liberation of this, even if it's also scary.

**Step 8:** Think of a situation in your life where you've been waiting for external guidance, a sign from God, or clarity about "the purpose" of that situation. Now shift: instead of waiting for external guidance, recognize that you are the consciousness that creates guidance. What does your inner knowing say? What would you choose if you fully owned your creative power?

**Step 9:** Repeat the vibration "Hü" (pronounced like the French "tu" or like "hue" without the hard "e") three times, letting it resonate from your ankles through your entire body. Feel how this sound activates the flexibility and creative potential within you.

**Step 10:** Speak the statement aloud with conviction: "I Am the Life Manifesting Ideas." Feel what this means. You are not just discovering ideas that already exist; you are the creative force bringing ideas into manifestation. You are consciousness creating reality through thought, belief, and choice.

**Step 11:** Visualize your path ahead not as a single narrow road predetermined by external force, but as infinite possibilities radiating out in all directions. Your ankles are strong and flexible, able to navigate any of these paths. Which way calls to you? Not which way "should" you go or which way God wants you to go, but which way does your deepest self choose?

**Step 12:** Make a commitment to yourself: "I release the need for an external God to tell me my purpose. I embrace my power and responsibility as consciousness creating meaning and choosing my path. I trust my inner knowing because I recognize it is not separate from the divine—it is the divine knowing itself through me."

### Closing

Slowly bring your awareness back to your breath and your physical surroundings. Stand and take a few steps, feeling your ankles flexing and adjusting, allowing you to move with grace and balance in whatever direction you choose.

Write in your journal about this experience. What was most challenging about this teaching? What was most liberating? Can you articulate a purpose that you are choosing to create rather than trying to discover? What meaning do you want to generate in your life?

This is deep work and it may take time to integrate. Be patient with yourself. You don't have to resolve all your beliefs about God or purpose today. Just allow this teaching to work in you, to open new possibilities of understanding.

## Integration

This teaching is profound and potentially destabilizing. Integration may take days, weeks, or longer. Be gentle with yourself as these concepts work through your consciousness.

Throughout the day, notice when you are waiting for external guidance, looking for signs from God, or trying to figure out "the purpose" of your life or a situation. In those moments, pause and shift: "What if there is no external answer to discover? What if I am the consciousness that creates the answer? What do I choose?"

Practice making small decisions purely from your own inner knowing without seeking external validation or divine confirmation. Start with simple things: what to eat, which route to take, how to spend your evening. Feel the freedom and responsibility of creating your own meaning and direction.

If you feel grief or loss around releasing your previous concept of God, honor that. You're not losing the divine; you're expanding your understanding of it. But the familiar image that gave you comfort is transforming, and it's okay to grieve that transition.

Journal about these questions: If there is no predetermined purpose for my life, what purpose do I choose to create? What matters most to me? How do I want to contribute to the exploration and evolution of consciousness? What meaning am I generating through my choices and actions?

Talk with trusted friends or spiritual companions about this teaching if that's helpful. Be discerning about who you share with—some people are not ready for this level of teaching and may react defensively. That's okay; everyone is on their own journey.

Notice moments when you feel the urge to explain or justify your existence, to prove you have a purpose. This is the old pattern of needing external validation. Practice simply being, creating, choosing without needing to justify yourself to any external authority.

## Q&A

**Q: If there's no God, what's the point of prayer or spiritual practice?**

**A:** This teaching doesn't say there's no God; it says the God we've imagined (external, separate, a being with a plan) doesn't exist. But consciousness itself, the divine totality, absolutely exists—and you are it. Prayer and spiritual practice shift from asking an external being for things to communing with your own deepest nature, aligning with the flow of consciousness, and manifesting your intentions through the creative power that you are. This is actually more powerful because you're working with reality directly rather than hoping for intervention from an imagined external source.

**Q: Without God's plan, how do I know if I'm making the right choices?**

**A:** There are no objectively "right" or "wrong" choices from the universe's perspective—only choices that lead to different experiences and learnings. However, you have inner guidance: your conscience, your intuition, your sense of alignment or misalignment. When a choice feels contracted, heavy, or dissonant with your deepest values, it's probably not aligned with your highest good. When a choice feels expansive, energizing, and aligned with love and truth, it's probably serving your evolution. You know. You've always known. You just haven't trusted yourself because you were taught to look outside for the answers.

**Q: Doesn't this teaching lead to selfishness and chaos if everyone just makes up their own purpose?**

**A:** No, because we are all expressions of the same consciousness, and that consciousness has a natural tendency toward connection, love, and evolution. When you truly recognize yourself as consciousness itself, you naturally care about all beings because you recognize they are you. Selfishness comes from the illusion of separation; when separation dissolves, selfishness dissolves with it. True spiritual maturity leads to greater compassion and service, not less, because you act from love itself rather than from fear and obligation.

**Q: What about all the people who have profound experiences of God as a being? Are they all deluded?**

**A:** Not deluded, but experiencing consciousness through the filter of their beliefs and cultural conditioning. If you deeply believe in and expect a father God, consciousness can and will meet you in that form because consciousness is infinitely creative and responsive. The experience is real; the interpretation may be limited. As we evolve, we can have direct experiences of consciousness itself without needing to anthropomorphize it or give it human qualities. Both levels of experience are valid for where a person is in their development.

**Q: I feel lost and scared without the belief in God's plan. How do I cope with this?**

**A:** This is a natural and important part of the transition. You're letting go of a security blanket, and there's a period of vulnerability before you realize you don't actually need it. Be compassionate with yourself. Take your time. You don't have to let go of everything at once. Work with this teaching gradually. Practice making small choices from your own knowing and see that you're actually quite capable. Over time, the fear transforms into freedom and empowerment. If needed, work with a therapist or spiritual guide who understands this level of teaching to support you through the transition.

**Q: How is this different from atheism?**

**A:** Atheism typically denies the existence of any divine reality and sees the universe as purely material and random. This teaching affirms that divine consciousness absolutely exists—in fact, it's the only thing that exists—but it's not a separate being "up there" with a plan. It's the totality of existence, the field of awareness in which all experience arises, the creative intelligence that manifests as all things. You are it; I am it; all that is, is it. This is closer to non-dual mysticism or panentheism than to atheism.

**Q: Can I still use traditional religious practices and language if they're meaningful to me?**

**A:** Absolutely. Understanding the deeper truth doesn't mean you have to abandon forms and practices that resonate with you. You can pray to God the Father while understanding at a deeper level that "God the Father" is a face that consciousness wears to meet you where you are. You can work with saints, angels, deities, and other spiritual beings while recognizing them as aspects of the one consciousness. The forms are tools; use whatever tools serve your connection to truth and love.

**Q: If I create my own purpose, what prevents me from choosing something harmful or destructive?**

**A:** Remember, you're not an isolated ego creating purpose in a vacuum. You are consciousness itself, and consciousness has an inherent intelligence and movement toward evolution, love, and integration. When you're truly connected to your deepest nature (not just your conditioned ego), you naturally choose purposes that serve the whole because you recognize yourself as the whole. Harmful or destructive purposes come from the illusion of separation and fear, not from aligned connection with consciousness. Also, you'll receive feedback: choices that truly misalign with consciousness create suffering, which is the universe's way of showing you that you're off track.

**Q: This seems to contradict many sacred texts and traditions. Are they all wrong?**

**A:** Not wrong, but expressed in the language and concepts available to the culture and time in which they arose. Sacred texts contain deep truth encoded in story, metaphor, and anthropomorphic imagery because that's how humans learn and connect with teaching. As consciousness evolves collectively, we're ready for more direct, less metaphorical teaching. The same truths that mystics have always known can now be expressed more plainly. The essence of all genuine spiritual teaching points to the same reality: you are not separate from the divine; you are it, knowing itself.
