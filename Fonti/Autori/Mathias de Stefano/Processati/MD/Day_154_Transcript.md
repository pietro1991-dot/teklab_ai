## Overview

On January 4th, we explore the Law of Rhythm—the fourth Hermetic principle. Matias teaches that everything flows in cycles, like a pendulum swinging back and forth, or the tide moving in and out. Nothing stays still. Everything moves in rhythm.

This law is deeply connected to the heart chakra because **the heart is the cosmic rhythm of the universe**—the heartbeat of existence itself. Just as our physical heart beats in a steady rhythm, the entire cosmos pulses in cycles: day and night, seasons, birth and death, expansion and contraction.

Understanding rhythm helps us stop fighting the natural flow of life. When we resist the cycles—wanting summer to last forever, or trying to avoid winter—we create suffering. **Rhythm teaches us to flow, to surrender, to trust the pendulum.** What goes up must come down. What goes out must come in. This is not a problem. This is the way.

## Core Teaching

### Everything Flows

The fourth Hermetic law is the Law of Rhythm.

**Everything flows.** Everything has a cycle. Everything moves in patterns of repetition.

Nothing in the universe is static. Everything moves in rhythm—back and forth, in and out, up and down.

### The Pendulum

The best way to understand rhythm is to imagine a pendulum.

A pendulum swings from one side to the other. It never stays in the center. It moves left, then right, then left again. Always in motion. Always in rhythm.

**This is how the universe works.**

Everything has a pendulum. Everything swings between polarities.

- Day swings to night. Night swings to day.
- Summer swings to winter. Winter swings to summer.
- Joy swings to sadness. Sadness swings to joy.
- Expansion swings to contraction. Contraction swings to expansion.

**This is not a problem. This is the rhythm.**

### The Heart: The Cosmic Rhythm

The heart chakra is the center of rhythm in the body.

**Your heartbeat is the rhythm of the universe.** Tick, tock. Tick, tock. Expansion, contraction. In, out.

The heart doesn't beat once and stop. It beats in a cycle. Over and over. That's rhythm.

And just like the heart, **the entire cosmos beats.** The universe is expanding and contracting in a cosmic heartbeat. Galaxies move in cycles. Planets orbit in rhythm. Stars are born and die in patterns.

**You are part of this cosmic heartbeat.** Your heart beats with the universe.

### Seasons and Cycles

One of the easiest ways to see rhythm is in the seasons.

Spring comes. Life grows. Energy expands. Everything blooms.

Summer arrives. Life peaks. Energy is at its fullest. Everything is alive and vibrant.

Autumn begins. Life slows. Energy contracts. Everything prepares to rest.

Winter settles. Life rests. Energy is dormant. Everything is still and quiet.

And then spring comes again. The cycle repeats.

**This is rhythm.**

We don't question the seasons. We don't say, "Why does winter have to come? I want summer to last forever."

**We accept it.** We flow with it. We trust the rhythm.

### Why Do We Resist Rhythm in Our Lives?

But when it comes to our own lives, we resist rhythm.

When we feel joy, we want it to last forever. We cling to it. We fear the sadness that will come.

When we feel sadness, we want it to end immediately. We push it away. We resist the natural cycle.

**But this is fighting the pendulum.** And when you fight the pendulum, you suffer.

**The pendulum will swing.** That's its nature. That's the law.

When you understand rhythm, you stop resisting. You flow. You trust.

"I'm in summer now. I feel joy. I'll enjoy it fully, knowing autumn will come."

"I'm in winter now. I feel sadness. I'll rest in it, knowing spring will return."

**This is wisdom. This is rhythm.**

### Tick Tock: The Heartbeat of Time

Matias uses the metaphor of a clock: **tick, tock.**

Tick—one side of the pendulum. Tock—the other side.

**The ticking is the rhythm.** It's constant. It's predictable. It's reliable.

You can count on it. The pendulum will swing. The heart will beat. The seasons will change.

**This is the comfort of rhythm.** It's not chaotic. It's not random. It's a pattern. And patterns create stability.

### Patterns in North America

Matias mentions that in North America, there's a cultural resistance to rhythm. People want things to stay the same. They want stability without change.

**But stability without rhythm is stagnation.**

True stability comes from trusting the rhythm. Trusting the cycle. Knowing that change is not a threat—it's the natural flow.

When you resist change, you create rigidity. And rigidity breaks.

When you flow with change, you create flexibility. And flexibility bends without breaking.

### The Astral Plane: The Emotional Rhythm

The teaching for today is connected to the Astral plane—the plane of the emotional body.

The word "astral" comes from **"astrum,"** which means **star.**

**The cosmos is a big illuminated body made of emotional lights—the souls.** Each soul is a star, glowing with emotion, moving in rhythm.

The Astral plane teaches us that **every soul is necessary.** Every individual is important to the whole. And the whole is important to the individual.

**This is the rhythm of relationship.** Give and take. Individual and collective. Me and we.

When you understand the Astral plane, you understand that **you are not separate from the cosmos.** Your emotions are part of the cosmic rhythm. Your joy and sadness are part of the universal heartbeat.

**You are not alone in your cycles.** You are moving with the whole.

### Repetition: The Comfort of Rhythm

Rhythm is about repetition.

**Repetition creates comfort.** When you know what's coming, you relax. You trust.

This is why rituals are powerful. This is why routines help. They create rhythm. They create predictability.

**But repetition is not about being stuck.** It's about flowing within a pattern.

The seasons repeat, but every spring is different. Every summer is unique. The pattern is the same, but the experience is new.

**This is the beauty of rhythm.** It's both familiar and fresh.

### The Stair to All Levels

The statement for today is: **"I Am the Stair to All Levels."**

What does this mean?

It means **you move through all the levels of the rhythm.** You experience all the cycles. You flow through all the seasons.

You don't stay stuck in summer. You don't hide from winter. **You move.** You are the stair—the bridge between all the levels.

You experience joy AND sadness. Expansion AND contraction. Growth AND rest.

**You are not one level. You are the movement through all levels.**

And because you move through all levels, you understand them all. You have compassion for all. You honor all.

This is the heart chakra. This is the Astral plane. **You are the stair to all levels.**

## Practice

### Preparation

1. Sit comfortably with your spine straight.
2. Place your hand on your heart.
3. Close your eyes and take a deep breath.

### Main Practice Steps

1. Feel your heartbeat. Notice the rhythm: **tick, tock, tick, tock.**

2. Your heart is the cosmic rhythm. It beats with the universe.

3. Take a slow breath in. Feel your chest expand. This is expansion—one side of the pendulum.

4. Take a slow breath out. Feel your chest contract. This is contraction—the other side of the pendulum.

5. Continue breathing. In, out. In, out. Feel the rhythm.

6. Now, imagine you are standing in front of a huge staircase with infinite levels.

7. Each level represents a different part of the rhythm:
   - Bottom level: Winter, rest, sadness, contraction.
   - Middle levels: Spring and autumn, transition, balance.
   - Top level: Summer, growth, joy, expansion.

8. You are standing in the middle. You are the bridge.

9. Take a step down. Feel yourself entering winter. Feel the stillness. The quiet. The rest. This is okay. This is part of the rhythm.

10. Now, take a step up. Feel yourself entering summer. Feel the energy. The growth. The joy. This is okay. This is part of the rhythm.

11. Move up and down the stairs. Notice the rhythm. Notice the cycles.

12. Realize: **you are not stuck in one season. You are the movement itself.**

13. Return to the center. Feel your heartbeat again: **tick, tock.**

14. Repeat: **"I Am the Stair to All Levels."**

15. **"I Am the Stair to All Levels."**

16. **"I Am the Stair to All Levels."**

17. Feel yourself as the rhythm. You are not resisting the pendulum. You are flowing with it.

18. You move through all levels. You honor all seasons. You trust the cycle.

19. Take a deep breath. Bring your awareness back to your heart.

20. Feel the cosmic heartbeat within you: **tick, tock, tick, tock.**

### Closing

1. Open your eyes slowly.
2. Remove your hand from your heart.
3. Return to the present moment, trusting the rhythm.

## Q&A

### Q: What is the Law of Rhythm?

The Law of Rhythm states that everything in the universe moves in cycles. Nothing stays static—everything flows back and forth like a pendulum. Day becomes night, summer becomes winter, joy becomes sadness and back again. This is not a problem; it's the natural rhythm of existence. Understanding this law helps us stop resisting the cycles and start flowing with them.

### Q: Why is the heart chakra connected to rhythm?

The heart is the cosmic rhythm of the universe. Your heartbeat—tick, tock—is the pattern of expansion and contraction that the entire cosmos follows. Just as your heart beats in a steady cycle, the universe pulses in rhythms: seasons, orbits, life and death. The heart chakra teaches us to trust this rhythm and flow with it.

### Q: How do the seasons represent rhythm?

The seasons are the clearest example of rhythm. Spring (growth), summer (peak), autumn (slowdown), winter (rest)—the cycle repeats endlessly. We don't resist the seasons; we accept them. But in our personal lives, we resist the natural cycles, wanting joy to last forever or pushing away sadness. Rhythm teaches us to honor all seasons equally.

### Q: What does "tick tock" symbolize?

"Tick tock" is the sound of the pendulum swinging, the heartbeat of time. Tick—one side of the cycle. Tock—the other side. It represents the constant, predictable, reliable rhythm of the universe. This repetition creates stability and comfort. You can trust it. The pendulum will always swing. The heart will always beat.

### Q: Why do we resist rhythm in our own lives?

We resist rhythm because we fear loss and change. When we feel joy, we want to cling to it forever. When we feel sadness, we want to push it away immediately. But this is fighting the natural flow. The pendulum will swing whether we resist or not. Resistance creates suffering. Acceptance creates peace.

### Q: What is the Astral plane?

The Astral plane is the level of consciousness connected to the emotional body. "Astral" comes from "astrum" (star). The cosmos is a vast illuminated body made of emotional lights—the souls. Each soul is necessary to the whole, and the whole is necessary to each soul. This plane teaches the rhythm of relationship: give and take, individual and collective, me and we.

### Q: What does "I Am the Stair to All Levels" mean?

It means you are not stuck in one part of the rhythm. You move through all cycles—joy and sadness, expansion and contraction, summer and winter. You are the bridge, the movement itself. You don't avoid any level; you experience them all. This gives you wisdom, compassion, and the ability to honor all seasons of life.

### Q: How can I flow with rhythm instead of resisting it?

First, recognize what cycle you're in. Ask: "Am I in expansion or contraction? Growth or rest?" Then, accept it without judgment. "I'm in winter. This is okay. I will rest." Trust that the pendulum will swing. Spring will come. You don't have to force it. Practice surrender: let the rhythm move you instead of trying to control it.
