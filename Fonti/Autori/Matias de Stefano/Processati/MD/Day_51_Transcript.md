# Day 51 - Be Born: The Grid of Ideas

## Overview

Day 51 marks the beginning of Virgo's **BODY** week, the third and final week of the Virgo constellation, focused on the physical grid—the energetic and geometric structure that underlies all manifestation. Today's teaching on **Be Born** explores the profound truth that birth and death are not separate events but continuous transformations within the eternal wave of existence. Matias reveals that the universe operates through constant vibration where nothing truly ends or begins; rather, everything transforms from one state to another, creating what we perceive as the cycle of life and death.

The Crown chakra connection on this day brings awareness to how our birth process—the very first pulse of our physical existence—determines the energetic pattern we carry throughout life. The concept of being "the Grid of Ideas" emphasizes that we are not random creations but intentional expressions of the spirit seeking to experience its own potential through physical form. The spirit, already enlightened, chooses to "die" into limitation so it may experience what it knows in its infinite wisdom.

This teaching dismantles the common belief that life is a temporary interruption between two voids. Instead, Matias presents the revolutionary understanding that consciousness expands continuously across dimensions, that our "soul" is not a single entity but an expansion of energy absorbing information from countless sources, and that what we call reincarnation is actually a constant birthing process happening simultaneously across all levels of reality. The practice of examining our own birth circumstances provides the key to understanding our life's impulse—the fundamental energetic pattern that pushes us forward or holds us back.

The invitation today is profound: to recognize that being born is not a one-time event but a choice we make every moment to manifest spirit into matter, to transform potential into experience, and to create the grid through which universal ideas become tangible reality.

## Core Teaching

### The Continuous Wave of Existence

Matias begins by establishing a fundamental truth: **"There is no death and life as a beginning and an end. That doesn't exist. It's just a perception that we have."** The universe functions as a constant wave where what we interpret as death in one reality is simultaneously birth in another. This is not metaphorical—it describes the actual mechanism through which consciousness navigates dimensions.

The analogy of ocean waves demonstrates this perfectly: "The waves break in the shore constantly, once and again, once and again. Never ending. So this is exactly how the universe works. When you die, you just—the process of dying in this reality is the process of being born in another reality." This continuous motion means rest and action are not determined by death and life, but by harmony and ceremony. Those who die in disharmony continue in action (becoming what we call "ghosts"), while those in harmony find rest regardless of their dimensional state.

### The Trinity of Spirit, Soul, and Body

A crucial clarification emerges about the three aspects of self: **"Spirit is vibration, soul is energy, and body is matter. The three of them are the same thing in different frequencies."** Like water existing as liquid, gas, and solid, these are not separate entities but expressions of the same essence at different vibrational rates.

The spirit, Matias explains, sees all possibilities but cannot experience them without creating intention. That intention becomes soul—energy in motion. The soul then creates patterns that shape matter, which becomes body. **"The spirit will be able to understand its own ideas through the energy of the soul by the experiences of the body."** This clarifies why we incarnate: not to achieve enlightenment (the spirit already IS enlightened), but to **experience** that light through limitation.

### The Birth Pulse: Programming Your Life Pattern

Perhaps the most practical revelation concerns how our actual birth circumstances encode our life pattern: **"To understand the way in which we were born helps us to understand the way we live our lives today."** Matias shares his personal example—born by C-section without struggle, never crying, just observing—which created his pattern of life coming easily without effort.

This isn't psychological interpretation but energetic imprinting. The first pulse, the way we enter physical reality, establishes the vibrational frequency that will characterize our approach to all life transitions: **"The people that had a difficult birth, for sure they will struggle in the process of changes during your life."**

Understanding this pulse grants power: **"When I recognize from where the things are coming from, I take power from the things that happen. And this is how I can recognize that I am the only one to decide how I want to live my new life."** By becoming conscious of our birth pattern, we can reprogram how we approach rebirth experiences throughout life.

### Reincarnation as Dimensional Expansion

The teaching on reincarnation revolutionizes common spiritual understanding. Reincarnation is not a linear process of human life → death → rest → return as human. Instead: **"The reincarnation process is a constant process. It's not just for the third dimension. So the concept of reincarnation would be like the concept of adapting to new dimensions."**

Even beings in the fifth dimension "die" when they realize knowledge gaps and must be "born" in the third dimension to experience what they missed. A master becomes a student. This means: **"When you die, the energy that makes us exist just expands in the environment and they just go by electricity and resonance to the trees, to animals, to the plants."**

Your consciousness doesn't go whole into one soul—it **expands** into many. This is why we remember lives we didn't actually live: **"My soul can remember to be a tree or, for example, can remember to be a Julius Caesar. And maybe I wasn't Julius Caesar, but maybe I died close by him. So I just felt my soul capture the information of him."**

### FA Code: The Cyclic Journey

The Soul Pattern FA represents the breath of cyclic transformation: **"The universe is an ocean that evaporated, turning into gaseous state, forming clouds that travel through the air, condensing into rain, unique raindrops that fall over mountains and plains, and into snow, each one unique and irrepeatable, identifying into ice until it melts, and flows on streams and rivers, back into the ocean."**

This poetic description encodes the actual process of consciousness: evaporation (death/spiritualization), cloud formation (soul gathering), rain (individualization), snow (crystallization of unique expression), ice (solid identity), melting (dissolution of form), flowing (return journey), ocean (source). FA is the sound of this never-ending cycle.

### Preparation: Birth Investigation

Before the meditation, engage in the crucial practice of **birth investigation**:

1. **Ask about your birth circumstances:**
   - Natural birth or surgery?
   - What was the context of your arrival?
   - Were you premature, on time, or late?
   - What was happening in your family/world?
   - Were you wanted, unexpected, adopted?

2. **If you don't remember, ask family members:**
   - What was told about your birth?
   - What were the first words about you?
   - How did you emerge?
   - Did you cry or stay quiet?

3. **If no one can tell you, meditate on it:**
   - Trust that your body remembers
   - Allow impressions to surface
   - Don't dismiss what arises
   - The subconscious holds all information

4. **Write down everything you discover:**
   - Create a detailed birth narrative
   - Note feelings, circumstances, reactions
   - Connect patterns to your current life
   - Acknowledge how your birth pulse operates today

## Key Insights

1. **"Nobody really dies. Nobody really is born. But everything is constantly transforming itself."** — This statement dismantles the illusion of beginnings and endings. What we experience as birth and death are transition points in an eternal wave. Understanding this removes fear of death and preciousness about life, allowing us to flow with transformation.

2. **"When we die, we don't go to rest. And then we take action when we start a new life. But it's not like that. When we find the resting process during the process of harmony."** — Rest is not a reward for death; it's a state of harmony achievable in any dimension. This means we can find deep rest while alive by achieving vibrational harmony, and we can remain in frantic action after death if we died in disharmony (explaining ghosts and restless spirits).

3. **"The spirit doesn't look for enlightenment because the spirit is already enlightened. What is the spirit trying to do then? To try to experience that light."** — This revolutionizes why we incarnate. We're not here to become something we're not; we're here to experience what we already are. The journey is not achievement but experience. This removes spiritual striving and replaces it with experiential presence.

4. **"In order for something to be born, something else must die. So picture this that in order for a life to exist in this wave, it needs to come from a death down and it goes to a death down again."** — Every birth requires a death; every creation requires a destruction. This is not tragedy but mechanics. To be born into a new job, old employment must die. To birth a new relationship, old patterns must die. Understanding this makes us willing participants in necessary endings.

5. **"The moment of being born is the specific moment that determines the energy with which we jump into life, the pulse of our life."** — Your birth circumstances are not just memories but active energetic programs. How you were born determines how you approach all life changes. This makes birth investigation a crucial spiritual practice, not therapy but energetic archaeology.

6. **"I was born by a surgery and doctor just took me out of my mom. I didn't push, didn't do anything, I was just there. And suddenly I just was looking around without crying. So that gave me the way, the concept of my life being so easy."** — Matias's personal example demonstrates how birth patterns encode life approaches. His easy birth created an easy life. This isn't psychology; it's vibrational programming. Your birth story is your life blueprint.

7. **"The consciousness when you die doesn't go and holds to the soul. The consciousness expands in the soul. So basically the soul doesn't catch all the consciousness. It just tries to expand."** — Death is not consolidation but expansion. Your consciousness doesn't contract into one soul package; it expands across multiple forms, places, beings. This explains why we have memories of lives we didn't live and why soul connections feel so vast.

8. **"For example, my soul can remember to be a tree or for example can remember to be a Julius Caesar. And maybe I wasn't Julius Caesar, but maybe I was, I died close by him. So I just felt my soul capture the information of him."** — Soul memory is not personal history but energetic absorption. When you died, your soul captured information from everything around you—people, places, events, entities. This is why past-life memories are often accurate but not "yours" in the personal sense.

9. **"Remember that spirit, soul and body are three different aspects of the self, of the same being. Different aspects, but the same being."** — The common error is treating spirit, soul, and body as separate entities that must be unified. They're already unified—just vibrating at different frequencies. Spirit = vibration, Soul = energy, Body = matter. Same substance, different states. Like ice, water, and steam.

10. **"The spirit is the ocean. The soul are the currents inside the ocean. And each one of the waves in the ocean will be life and death, life and death of different bodies. But everything is a continuous. It just transforms itself."** — This metaphor perfectly encodes the relationship between eternal spirit, flowing soul, and wave-like bodies. The ocean never stops being ocean whether it's calm, stormy, or frozen. Similarly, spirit never stops being spirit whether manifesting as soul currents or body waves.

11. **"So this is why in order for something to be born, something else must die. So to be born is what makes us remember that we are the expression of the spirit trying to experience the knowledge and the wisdom that it has within."** — Birth is remembrance, not beginning. Every time we're born (whether into physical life or a new phase), we're remembering our purpose: to be the vehicle through which spirit experiences itself. This makes every birth sacred and intentional.

12. **"How was your birth? So this is why the task for today is to acknowledge, to be aware of everything that you have lived during the process of being born."** — The question that unlocks your life pattern. Not "what do you remember?" but "how were you born?" because the how determines the what of your entire life. This question is not therapeutic excavation but energetic investigation.

13. **"I was in a natural birth, I was born in a surgery, I was born in which context, I was abandoned, I was nobody told me how it was. I just don't remember anything."** — Whatever your birth circumstances, they hold the key. Surgical birth teaches one pattern, natural birth another. Abandoned at birth creates different wiring than celebrated arrival. Unknown origins generate their own unique frequency. No birth is wrong; each is informative.

14. **"The people that had a difficult birth, for sure they will struggle in the process of changes during your life."** — This is not determinism but pattern recognition. Difficult birth = struggle pattern with transitions. Easy birth = ease pattern with changes. Knowing this allows reprogramming: "I struggled to be born, so I expect struggle with change. But I can consciously choose ease now."

15. **"When I recognize from where the things are coming from, I take power from the things that happen. And this is how I can recognize that I am the only one to decide how I want to live my new life."** — The power statement of the teaching. Recognizing the source (birth pattern) gives you power over its expression. You're not victim to your birth circumstances; you're informed by them. And information creates choice. Choice creates new birth.

## Questions & Answers

**Q: "If death and life are continuous waves, why do we experience them as separate and traumatic events?"**

**A:** Because we're identifying with the wave instead of the ocean. Matias explains: "If it's I, me, it's just I could—it's another projection. It's another projection of the reality." When we identify as the individual wave (the "I"), death feels like annihilation. But when we recognize ourselves as the ocean (the "I AM"), we see that waves rise and fall continuously without the ocean ever dying. The trauma comes from the illusion of separation. Once you recognize "I am not just the wave; I am the ocean waving," death becomes transition, not termination. The practice is to expand identity beyond the form—to know yourself as the continuous vibration expressing through many forms.

**Q: "You say I should investigate my birth, but I was adopted and truly don't know anything about my actual birth circumstances. What do I do?"**

**A:** Matias addresses this directly: "One of you may be were adopted, nobody spoke about this issue. I don't know, it's one is different. And if you don't know anything, acknowledge that. Like no one ever told me how I was born." The unknowing IS your pattern. It creates a specific energetic signature: mystery, disconnection from origin, need to create your own story. This becomes your life pattern—you may approach many situations without clear origin information, needing to trust inner knowing over outer confirmation. Meditate on the egg and spermatozoid union; your BODY remembers even if your mind doesn't know the story. Trust cellular memory. And recognize: not knowing your birth story is PART of your birth story. It creates specific gifts and challenges that shape your path.

**Q: "How can reincarnation be 'continuous' across dimensions when I feel like a distinct individual with one linear life?"**

**A:** The teaching challenges this perception. Matias says: "When you die, your consciousness is not whole in one soul. It expands in many." You are not one distinct individual; you are a focusing point of consciousness that has gathered information from countless sources. Right now, reading this, "you" are a temporary constellation of memories, some from your direct experience, many absorbed from your environment, ancestors, collective consciousness. When this body dies, this particular constellation dissolves, and the consciousness expands—some goes to become trees, some to other humans, some to higher dimensional learning. There is no "you" that remains intact and individual. There is consciousness experiencing itself through temporary focus points called "individuals." Your distinct identity is real as an experience but illusory as a permanent entity. Like a wave is real while it's formed but not separate from the ocean.

**Q: "If the spirit is already enlightened and doesn't need to evolve, why does it bother creating all this suffering and limitation?"**

**A:** This question assumes suffering and limitation are problems to the spirit, but Matias reveals: "What is the spirit trying to do then? To try to experience that light." The spirit knows everything but has never EXPERIENCED knowing. Imagine having all knowledge about chocolate but never tasting it. The spirit descends into limitation not because it needs to learn but because it wants to FEEL what it knows. Suffering isn't a mistake; it's contrast that creates the capacity for experience. Without darkness, light can't be experienced. Without limitation, freedom is just a concept. The spirit voluntarily "dies" into form specifically to experience the journey from ignorance back to knowing—not because it needs to make that journey, but because the journey itself is the point. Experience, not achievement, is the goal.

**Q: "My birth was traumatic—long labor, emergency intervention, my mother nearly died. Does this mean I'm destined for a difficult life?"**

**A:** No, it means you have a specific energetic pattern to work WITH, not be victimized BY. Matias teaches: "When I recognize from where the things are coming from, I take power from the things that happen." Your traumatic birth created certain wiring: perhaps you expect life changes to be dangerous, requiring emergency intervention. Perhaps you learned early that survival means fighting. KNOWING this is power. Now you can consciously choose: "Yes, I was born through struggle, AND I can choose to birth myself differently into new experiences." The pattern is information, not destiny. It will show up (you'll likely expect difficulty with transitions) but you can reprogram in real-time: "I see my pattern activating. I'm expecting this change to be traumatic. But I CHOOSE ease. I consciously birth myself into this moment with grace." Pattern is tendency, not fate.

**Q: "You said our soul captures information from people we died near. How do I know which past-life memories are actually mine versus absorbed from others nearby?"**

**A:** This question itself reveals a misunderstanding. Matias explains: "So this is why so many people remember to be important people in the past because when you died in the past, you always had idols." There is no "actually mine" versus "absorbed from others." ALL memories are absorptions. Even your current life memories are information absorbed from experiences that happened to this body-mind complex you call "you." The consciousness that animates you now has no more ownership of those memories than it does of the Julius Caesar memories absorbed from dying near him. The key insight: "And also it doesn't matter because the universe is just one. So we are all one. So it doesn't matter who you were." Don't try to sort "real" from "absorbed" memories. Instead, ask: "What information does this memory—wherever it came from—have for me now?" Use it all. It's all yours because there's no separate "you" to own it exclusively.

**Q: "How can I tell if I'm actually experiencing a new birth/rebirth or just repeating the same pattern with different circumstances?"**

**A:** Excellent discernment question. Check these markers: **(1) Energetic signature:** Does the new situation FEEL fundamentally different in your body, or does it feel familiar despite different content? Same anxiety/excitement/hesitation = same pattern. Genuinely new sensation = new birth. **(2) Response pattern:** Are you responding how you always do (avoid, attack, freeze, rush) or are you surprising yourself with new responses? New responses indicate new birth. **(3) Expectation check:** Before entering the situation, what did you expect? If it matched your usual expectations (it'll be hard, easy, confusing, clear), same pattern. If you had no expectations or different ones, new territory. **(4) Integration speed:** Old patterns integrate quickly (feels familiar). New births take time to integrate (feels awkward, uncertain, fresh). Welcome the awkwardness—it signals genuine newness. **(5) Ask directly:** "Spirit, am I being born new here or repeating?" Then listen. Your body knows. Trust the response.

**Q: "What's the practical difference between knowing I'm the Grid of Ideas versus just having ideas?"**

**A:** Having ideas means receiving thoughts that float through consciousness—you're passive receiver. Being the Grid of Ideas means you ARE the geometric structure through which universal ideation manifests into form—you're active creator. The difference: **Having ideas:** "I have an idea for a book." (Idea as possession) **Being the Grid:** "An idea for a book is trying to birth through the grid of my consciousness." (You as birthing channel) This shift changes everything. Having ideas = accumulation, ownership, personal achievement. Being the Grid = service, transmission, universal creativity flowing through you. The practical application: Stop trying to generate ideas (ego effort). Start recognizing yourself as the structure through which ideas birth themselves (grid function). Your job isn't to create ideas but to maintain a clear, coherent grid so ideas can birth through you without distortion. This is why the crown chakra connects today—the crown is the entry point where ideas from spirit enter the grid of your consciousness to birth into mental, emotional, and physical form.
