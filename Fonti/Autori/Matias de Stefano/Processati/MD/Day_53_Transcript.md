# Day 53 - Apprehend: The Grid of Vibrations

## Overview

Day 53 brings us to **Apprehend** through the Throat chakra—the center of expression, communication, and the **Law of Vibration**. This teaching dismantles the modern confusion between learning (mental accumulation) and apprehending (bodily absorption), revealing that true transformation happens not through studying but through **physical action** that encodes information into our cellular structure.

Matias traces "apprehend" to its Latin roots: *ad* (to go outside toward) + *prehendere* (to catch/grab)—revealing apprehension as the movement of going outside ourselves to catch what we need and bring it inside, making it our own. This is radically different from learning, which merely stores information mentally. **Apprehension is embodiment**—taking external patterns and encoding them so deeply into the body that they become automatic, unconscious, and inseparable from who we are.

The Throat chakra connection is profound: what we express outward comes back to us through vibration (the Law of Resonance). Everything in the universe vibrates, and vibrations that match our frequency return to us like echoes. This is the true "law of attraction"—not positive thinking, but **vibrational matching**. What you **vibrate** (not what you think) determines what resonates back to you.

The teaching emphasizes the first seven years of life as the crucial apprehension period, where we absorb language, culture, movement patterns, emotional responses, and ways of being through **imitation and physical repetition**, not conscious study. The first word you spoke, the way you learned to walk, the attitudes you mimicked from family—these are apprehensions that still govern your life because they're encoded at the cellular, not mental, level.

The revolutionary insight: **the mind learns, but the body apprehends**. Meditation and mental practices help organize information, but actual transformation requires physical action—doing, moving, practicing, repeating until the new pattern becomes bodily knowledge. This is why spiritual growth cannot happen through meditation alone; it requires embodied practice that trains the physical body to vibrate differently.

## Core Teaching

### Apprehend vs. Learn: The Critical Distinction

Matias begins with essential linguistic clarity: **"Apprehension comes from the Latin word *ad*, which is to go outside toward, and *prehendere*, which means to catch, to grab."** The word itself encodes the process: moving outward to grasp what you need and bring it into yourself.

The English word "learn" shares this root through the Saxon *leornian* (to catch/grab), but modern usage has separated learning from apprehension. **"To learn means to acquire something by constant repetition and by imitation, the mimic of it, but memorizing this concept."** Learning stores information; apprehension **embodies** it.

The distinction: **"The difference between apprehend and learn is, for example, that in the process of learning, we would say America was colonized in 1492. But that's just information. It's not my own. It's not, I didn't apprehend that. It's not useful for my life. But it's something that I just memorize to know it."**

True apprehension happens through body: **"To apprehend is to take stuff that will be useful for my daily life inside of me... So we would say that the mind learned, but the body apprehend."** This is revolutionary—your body holds the actual knowledge that shapes your life, while your mind merely stores data.

### The Law of Vibration: Resonance and Echo

The Throat chakra connects to the **Law of Vibration**, which Matias explains through sound: **"When I speak, when I communicate, I also receive the answers back. So I am learning by the environment... Everything that is around me, everything that rebounds with me is because I am related to everything through vibration."**

The mechanism: **"When we say a word, when we express something, we emit a vibration that moves the waves of vibration around me and like a sonar in a boat, it will rebound all the vibration and come back to me as an echo."** This is *resounding*—when sound comes back to you again.

This explains the true law of attraction: **"Everything that comes to me is because it is vibrating in the same level as I am by vibration... This is why everything that I need or I express for myself, all of that is going to come to me again."**

But here's the crucial correction to popular manifestation teaching: **"We usually have to think that attraction comes from vibration. So we may know a lot of people that even if they think in a good way, they don't vibrate in a good way."** The example: people who believe in being good but constantly gossip and criticize. **"To think is not the same as vibrate. So this is why the law of attraction works only about how I vibrate in my life and not how I think in my life."**

### The First Seven Years: Apprehension Period

The critical developmental window: **"The process of apprehension is something that we all do in the first seven years of our lives. When we are trying to adapt to the environment, so we take all these things that the environment gives us in order to allow ourselves to move and to expand and transcend in that space."**

During this period, everything is apprehended through physical absorption: **"In the first years of our lives, what we do is to accept from the external many of the things that we naturalized and normalize through our lives. But that they were not ours—like the language we speak, the actions we take, the attitudes that we use, the attributes that maybe we imitate from others in our family or environment, the culture that we are thinking."**

This is not conscious learning but **bodily absorption**: "What we apprehend is the ability to accept as our own many of the things that helps us be where we are." The body doesn't discriminate or analyze; it simply absorbs what's present and makes it cellular knowledge.

### Teeth: The First Processors

Matias introduces teeth as symbolic processors: **"The first thing that I do in the process is, for example, taking an apple, I see the apple, but then the first thing is to bite the apple and to start to chew the apple until it starts to go in pieces. So it's easy for the digestive system to process that information. Because the first process that we do is with the teeth."**

Each tooth represents an aspect of life where we process information: **"The teeth are the first extensions of our whole body that is trying to process information. So the teeth, each one of them represent an aspect of our life in which we try to process information."**

Dental problems indicate where processing difficulties exist: **"The problem that I have in each tooth would be something that can show me where is my problem of processing information. This is why the teeth are the first filter from where I can understand from where I am preventing information."**

### Mind Organizes, Body Transforms

The revolutionary teaching on meditation versus action: **"In order to apprehend something new, to do something different, I have to take action, to do it with my body, to act in my life. This is why the great changes of our lives cannot be done just by doing meditation."**

Meditation's role: **"Meditation would help us to order the physical, to think about the physical, to think about the issues, but then we have to take action. We have to do stuff. We have to work physically to acquire that information really within."**

This explains why many spiritual practitioners remain stuck despite extensive meditation practice—they're learning (mental) but not apprehending (physical). **"The physical body is the one able to apprehend. The information gets imprinted in our cells, in our body, in our DNA."**

### The First Word: Your Vibrational Signature

The practice centers on recovering your first spoken word: **"The task for today is to write down about what are the first learning, the first processes of apprehension that you had in your life. So we are going to start this by writing down what was the first word that we ever said in our lives."**

Why does this matter? Your first word represents your first conscious vibrational expression—the first time you actively sent sound into the world and experienced it returning. This establishes your relationship with the Law of Vibration that continues throughout life.

Following the first word: **"All the things that you could remember that you have learned in your first seven years of life, not after the seven years, before."** These early apprehensions—learning to walk, write, interact, respond—are still operative in your cellular structure today.

### Soul Pattern V: The Luggage of Inheritance

The Soul Pattern V teaching deepens: **"The only thing we take when we die is the inheritance of what we have lived, learned, experienced. Every being is a legacy itself, and memory is the only great success that we earn at the end of every life."**

What we apprehend becomes soul inheritance: **"V is the sound of what we have accomplished in life, the luggage we receive and we deliver, the concept of the soul's home."** Unlike learning (which dies with the brain), apprehension (encoded in body/soul) transfers across lifetimes.

This is why early childhood apprehensions are so powerful—they're not just this-life programming but often continuations of soul patterns from previous embodiments.

## Questions & Answers

**Q: "I've been meditating for years but don't feel transformed. What am I doing wrong?"**

**A:** You're not doing anything wrong—you're just learning, not apprehending. Matias is clear: **"The great changes of our lives cannot be done just by doing meditation."** Meditation organizes information, creates clarity, calms mind. Excellent. But **transformation happens through action**—through body apprehending new patterns via repetition and practice. Your meditation is the blueprint; now build the structure through physical practice. Meditate on compassion, then **act** compassionately until your body apprehends it. Meditate on courage, then **do** courageous things until cells encode it. Meditation + Action = Transformation. Meditation alone = Pleasant stagnation.

**Q: "How can I tell if something is apprehended in my body versus just learned in my mind?"**

**A:** Test it under stress. Learned knowledge requires conscious access—you have to think about it. Apprehended knowledge is automatic—body just does it. Example: you've learned "I should breathe deeply when anxious" (mental knowledge). But when actually anxious, do you? If no = learned but not apprehended. If body automatically deepens breath without thought = apprehended. Another test: Can you do it while distracted/tired/overwhelmed? Apprehension operates regardless of mental state. Learning requires mental bandwidth. What your body does automatically without thought = apprehended. Everything else = merely learned.

**Q: "My first word was 'no.' What does this mean for my vibrational signature?"**

**A:** Your first intentional vibration sent into reality was negation/boundary/refusal. This likely established your relationship with expression as protective, defensive, or limit-setting. Notice: Do you default to "no" before "yes"? Do you lead with what you don't want rather than what you do? Do you find it easier to refuse than accept? This isn't bad—boundaries are necessary. But if it's limiting you, consciously practice vibrating "yes" (to safe things) until body apprehends receptivity as equally valid expression. Your first word set a pattern; awareness lets you expand beyond it while honoring its protective wisdom.

**Q: "I don't remember my first seven years clearly. How can I identify early apprehensions?"**

**A:** Look at your automatic responses NOW—they reveal early apprehensions. When stressed, what do you do automatically? That's apprehended pattern. How do you instinctively respond to: authority figures, chaos, criticism, praise, conflict, intimacy? Those instant reactions are early apprehensions operating. You don't need memory of learning them; their presence proves they're there. Notice the pattern, trace it back: "Where would I have learned this?" Family system usually. Also: ask relatives, look at family photos, notice what triggers body responses. The body remembers even when mind doesn't.

**Q: "You say vibration matters more than thought, but aren't thoughts vibrations too?"**

**A:** Yes, but thoughts are **subtle** vibrations; bodily vibrations are **dense**. Both matter, but physical vibration (how your cells, nervous system, organs actually vibrate) has more material impact than mental vibration (thoughts). This is why someone can think positively (mental vibration) while their body vibrates stress/fear (physical vibration)—and the body wins every time in terms of what manifests. Align them: feel the thought in your body, let it become cellular vibration, then both levels work together. But when they conflict, body-vibration trumps thought-vibration in manifestation.

**Q: "How long does it take to apprehend a new pattern versus just learning it?"**

**A:** Learning happens instantly—you can read a concept and know it immediately. Apprehension requires repetition until it's cellular. Neuroplasticity research suggests 21-66 days of consistent practice to encode new neural/physical patterns. But this varies: Simple physical skill might apprehend in days (body learns fast). Complex emotional pattern might need months or years of practice. The marker: when you don't have to think about it anymore—when body just does it automatically under any conditions—it's apprehended. Don't rush. Deep apprehension is worth the time investment.

**Q: "If I apprehended harmful patterns (abuse, fear) early, am I stuck with them?"**

**A:** Absolutely not. Matias shows the path: **recognize the apprehension** (awareness), then **practice new patterns physically** (re-apprehension). You learned to walk, then learned to run—both apprehended through practice. You can re-apprehend anything. The key: you cannot merely think your way out (that's learning). You must practice new behaviors repeatedly until body apprehends them. Example: apprehended "people hurt you" from abuse → practice safe connections repeatedly until body apprehends "some people are safe." It takes time, practice, patience—but re-apprehension is absolutely possible. Your cells can learn new patterns at any age.

**Q: "What's the relationship between the Grid of Vibrations and the Law of Attraction?"**

**A:** The Grid of Vibrations IS the actual mechanism behind what's popularly called "law of attraction." The grid is the vibrational field you exist within and emit from. Law of Attraction should be called Law of Vibration: **"Everything that comes to me is because it is vibrating in the same level as I am by vibration."** You don't attract through thought; you resonate through vibration. Your physical/emotional/energetic vibration creates a frequency signature. Reality responds by sending matching frequencies back (like echo/resonance). Change your vibration (through apprehension/practice) and different things resonate with you. The grid is the resonance field; you are a vibrating node within it.

**Q: "Why focus on throat chakra for apprehension when it seems more physical/body-based?"**

**A:** Because apprehension happens through **vibration**, and throat is the vibration/expression center. You apprehend through: (1) Receiving vibrations from environment (listening, absorbing), (2) Testing them through expression (speaking, trying), (3) Feeling what echoes back (resonance). This is throat chakra function—the bidirectional flow of vibrational information. Also: early childhood apprehension heavily involves language/communication learning, which is throat-centered. The throat is where inner vibration meets outer vibration—perfect chakra for understanding how we apprehend reality's vibrational patterns.
