## 1. PRODUCT FAMILY OVERVIEW

### TK3+ Series Description

The **TK3+ Series** is a family of automatic oil level regulators combining electro-optic level detection with an integrated solenoid valve. Available in three pressure variants to cover standard refrigeration through CO2 transcritical applications.

**Core Technology:**
- Electro-optic IR sensor (fused glass hermetic seal)
- Integrated NC solenoid valve
- Automatic operation (no external controller needed)
- Factory programmed and calibrated
- Dual LEFT/RIGHT mounting capability
- Two-part serviceable design

### 6.4 Service & Maintenance

**Routine Maintenance:**
- **Quarterly**: Visual inspection (check for leaks, verify LED indication)
- **Annually**: Functional test (verify valve operation, relay output)
- **No scheduled component replacement** (replace only if failed)

**Electronic Module Replacement (5-10 minutes, no depressurization):**
1. Disconnect power (lockout/tagout)
2. Remove mounting screws (typically 2-4 screws)
3. Lift electronic module straight up (disconnect internal connector)
4. Install new module (align connector, press down gently)
5. Secure with mounting screws
6. Reconnect power and verify operation

**Valve Coil Replacement (5-10 minutes, no depressurization):**
1. Disconnect power
2. Remove coil retaining clip or screws
3. Slide coil off valve stem
4. Install new coil (align with valve stem)
5. Secure retaining hardware
6. Reconnect power and verify operation

**Complete Sensor/Valve Body Replacement (requires depressurization):**
1. Isolate compressor and oil return line
2. Recover refrigerant from crankcase (if necessary)
3. Remove electronic module
4. Disconnect flange bolts
5. Remove sensor/valve assembly
6. Install new assembly with new gasket/O-ring
7. Reconnect oil return line
8. Reinstall electronic module
9. Evacuate and test for leaks
10. Return to service

### 6.5 Troubleshooting

**Alarm Activates Immediately After Startup:**
- **Cause**: Oil separator empty or pressure too low
- **Solution**: Verify separator oil level, check separator pressure adequate (> 5 bar above crankcase)

**Valve Won't Open (Red LED, no oil flow):**
- **Cause**: Coil failure, wiring issue, insufficient differential pressure
- **Solution**: Check power supply, verify coil voltage, measure MOPD (need > 5 bar minimum)

**Continuous Filling (Yellow LED stays on):**
- **Cause**: Oil return line restricted, valve stuck open
- **Solution**: Check for blockage, verify line sizing adequate, inspect valve seat

**False Alarms (Intermittent Red LED):**
- **Cause**: Foam in crankcase, low refrigerant charge, undersized oil separator
- **Solution**: Check refrigerant charge, verify separator size adequate, check for liquid slugging

**No LED Indication:**
- **Cause**: Power supply failure, electronic module failure
- **Solution**: Verify power supply voltage at terminals, check fuse, replace module if needed

**Q1: What is TK3+ - sensor or regulator?**
TK3+ is an **oil level regulator** (not just a sensor). It combines electro-optic level detection with an integrated solenoid valve and control electronics for automatic oil level regulation.

**Q14: What maintenance is required?**
- **Quarterly**: Visual inspection (check for leaks, verify LED indication)
- **Annually**: Functional test (verify valve operation, relay output)
- **No scheduled component replacement** (only replace if failed)

### 7.5 Pressure & Applications

**Q15: Which TK3+ variant for CO2 systems?**
- **CO2 subcritical** (differential < 26 bar): TK3+ 46 bar (rare, typically need 80 bar)
- **CO2 subcritical** (differential 30-50 bar): TK3+ 80 bar
- **CO2 transcritical** (differential > 50 bar): TK3+ 130 bar

**Q16: Can I use TK3+ 46 bar for CO2 subcritical?**
Only if differential < 26 bar (rare). Most CO2 subcritical systems require TK3+ 80 bar due to 40-50 bar typical differentials.

**Q17: R410A hot climate - which model?**
R410A at 50Â°C condensing creates ~30 bar differential. Use **TK3+ 80 bar** for safety margin (MOPD 52 bar). TK3+ 46 bar works but operates at maximum rating (less safe).

**Q18: What does "46 bar / 80 bar / 130 bar" mean?**
This is the **maximum working pressure** the TK3+ can withstand. It refers to the separator/crankcase pressure, NOT the differential.

**Example**: TK3+ 80 bar can handle 70 bar separator pressure with 20 bar crankcase (50 bar differential, within 52 bar MOPD).

### 7.6 Multi-Compressor Systems

**Q19: Can I use TK3+ for multiple compressors?**
Yes. Install one TK3+ per compressor. Each operates independently.

**Power**: 3 compressors = 60VA (use 100VA transformer), 6 compressors = 120VA (use 150VA transformer)

**Q20: Wire relays in series or parallel?**
**Series** (for safety chain): All NC contacts in series with compressor contactors. Any TK3+ alarm â†’ stops all compressors.

### 7.7 Troubleshooting

**Q21: Valve won't open - what to check?**
- Power supply connected and correct voltage?
- Check LED indication (should show yellow when filling)
- Verify oil supply pressure differential adequate (need > 5 bar minimum)
- Check for blockage in oil return line
- Coil may be failed (replace coil without depressurizing)

**Q22: Alarm activates immediately**
- Oil separator empty or pressure too low
- Oil return line blocked
- Pressure differential insufficient (< 5 bar)
- Valve stuck closed (service valve)

**Q23: How to verify correct operation?**
- **Green LED**: Normal (oil level adequate, valve closed)
- **Yellow LED**: Filling (valve open, oil flowing)
- **Red LED**: Alarm (low oil persists)
- **Relay**: NC contact closed during normal, opens on alarm
- **Oil level**: Should stabilize and remain steady

### 7.8 Refrigerant Compatibility

**Q24: What refrigerants work with TK3+?**
- **TK3+ 46 bar**: R134a, R404A, R407C, R410A, R290, R600a, CO2 subcritical (if MOPD < 26 bar), Ammonia (approval required)
- **TK3+ 80 bar**: All above + CO2 subcritical, R410A hot climate
- **TK3+ 130 bar**: All above + CO2 transcritical

**Q25: What oils work with TK3+?**
All oils: Mineral, POE, PAG, AB, and all synthetic oils. Fused glass and nickel-plated steel provide universal compatibility.

**Q26: Can I use TK3+ with Ammonia?**
Consult Teklab engineering for approval. Materials compatible but application review recommended.
